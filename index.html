Features

Built for the moments you hope never come.

Four delivery modes. Each one designed around a different kind of fear.

Pre-flight lock

Record before you board. Seal until you land.

Set a check-in window. If you confirm your safe arrival, the message is automatically destroyed, unseen. If you don't — it delivers. No intervention needed.

TRIGGER: Inactivity past your check-in deadline  ·  Default: 48hrs after departure
Unlock if I don't return

Set a date. Miss it. It goes.

Choose a check-in date — a day you commit to marking yourself safe. If that day passes in silence, LAST releases your message to the contacts you chose. No manual intervention. No intermediary needed.

TRIGGER: No check-in by set date  ·  Sends to: up to 3 trusted contacts
Time-locked vault

Deliver on a date, years from now.

A message for their 18th birthday. An anniversary you won't be alive to see. A confession timed for when they're finally ready to hear it. Set it. Forget it. Trust it.

TRIGGER: Calendar date  ·  Storage: Permanent  ·  Encrypted at rest
Just in case

The message for the unthinkable.

No date. No flight. No surgery. Just a sealed message held in trust — released only if your account goes inactive for an extended period. For the death you can't schedule.

TRIGGER: Extended account inactivity  ·  Trusted contacts notified
Why these moments

Fear doesn't make us irrational.
It makes us honest.

Terror Management Theory: when people are reminded of death, they act with urgency on the things they actually value. Not what they planned to value — what they do value. LAST works because it meets you at that exact threshold.

✈️

The pre-flight moment

Boarding a flight activates what researchers call "mortality salience." You become temporarily honest. That's the moment to capture something real — before the plane lands and the armour goes back on.

💊

The surgery window

Patients report heightened emotional clarity in the hours before going under. The consent forms strip away denial. Unfinished conversations become physically unbearable.

🗻

The risk threshold

People who engage in high-risk activities consistently report wanting to leave something behind — not as morbid ritual, but as an act of love. The climb becomes meaningful when it's anchored to the people waiting at the bottom.

🌙

The night before

Major life transitions — diagnosis, divorce, departure — compress our sense of time. Tomorrow feels discontinuous from today. People instinctively want to document who they were before the rupture.
